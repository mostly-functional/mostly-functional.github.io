Introduction

While there are mountains of resources for learning about any given tech-related topic out there, there’s a lack of good, resources that present everything you’re “supposed” to know as someone working in a modern, tech-oriented organization. This document is meant to fill this gap.

It presents the fundamentals of a number of tech-related topics that anyone working in a modern tech organization should be familiar with. It is intended to be used both as a guide for self-study, and as also as a starting point for more in-depth study on any given topic.

What it is not meant to be is a comprehensive guide to any given topic — rather, the aim is to provide the reader with a fundamental understanding of core concepts. For topics where most people don’t need a deep understanding, we provide a summary of the most important concepts and information. For subjects where in-depth knowledge is important for some roles, we provide a brief summary of the subject (for everybody), as well as directions on how to learn the subject and references to more comprehensive material (for those who want to learn the subject in depth).

Throughout, we try to include Wikipedia references to any terms that could be unfamiliar, as well as provide references to useful tools and resources for more in-depth study.

You’re encouraged to study this material at your own leisure. It’s written in a “maximally information-dense” style to facilitate use as a reference — so don’t expect to understand everything at once! Rather, approach it on your own terms, and feel free to skim the parts that seem less relevant to you.

Good luck! :)
